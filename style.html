02. The Landscape

The "Closet Full of Nothing" Paradox

The Global Fashion Tech market is valued at $33 Billion. Yet, average consumer utilizes less than 20% of their wardrobe. This is not a supply problem; it is a cognitive load problem.

Enclothed Cognition

"The scientific principle that clothes we wear affect our behavioral processes and confidence. When a user feels their outfit doesn't match their intent, they experience 'Style Anxiety'."

The Opportunity: Users don't need another shopping app. They need a tool that bridges gap between Inventory (what they have) and Context (where they are going).

07. The Pivot

Why "Virtual Try-On" Failed

The Hypothesis: Users want to see the clothes on a 3D avatar to judge fit.

The Reality: During usability testing (Mid-Fi), the avatar feature caused a 40% drop-off rate.

*Specific Data: 2 out of 5 participants abandoned the task entirely, citing "Uncanny Valley" discomfort.

"It looks creepy. That's not my body. I'd rather just see the clothes flat."
— Test Participant P3

The Fix: I scrapped the 3D avatar entirely. I pivoted to a "Digital Twin" logic where the app asks for specific body measurements and uses text-based validation.

The Result: Trust scores increased by 60% in the second round of testing.

*Comparative Metric: Average "Likelihood to Rely on Recommendation" rating rose from 2.5/5 (V1) to 4.0/5 (V2).
