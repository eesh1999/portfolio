01 / The Why

Why I started this journey.

In India, we choose our career path at age 15 based on marks, not passion. I realized students were paralyzed by "Netflix Paralysis," while parents were terrified of non-traditional careers. I saw a system that prioritized rote memory over true aptitude.

02 / Research Methodology

How I learned by
asking "Why?"

I sat down with 15 students to understand the root of the "Choice Crisis." It wasn't just about jobs; it was about the Trust Gap between two generations.

Q1: WHY ENGINEERING?

"Because my marks in Math were 95%."

Q2: WHY DO MARKS DEFINE THE CHOICE?

"My parents think marks translate to career safety."

Q3: WHY IS SAFETY THE TOP PRIORITY?

"They don't know if creative fields like Design pay well."

THE BREAKTHROUGH INSIGHT

Aptitude is invisible until practiced. I needed to make student aptitude visible to parents through tangible data and physical proof.

04 / My Evolution Circuit

What I got wrong.

Testing with real humans forced me to scrap features I initially loved. Here is the logic circuit of my pivots.

MY ASSUMPTION

Students wanted a separate "Job Dictionary" to read about salaries first.

MY CORRECTION

No one read the text. I integrated all data inside POV Quest Cards. Context-first discovery.

MY ASSUMPTION

Facial Sentiment AI would be the best way to track student excitement.

MY CORRECTION

Students felt "Spied on." I switched to Behavioral Voice AI. Less invasive, higher trust.

05 / The System I built

Why "Construction Chic"?

I rejected the boring "Corporate Blue" of most apps. I wanted a visual language that screamed "Building" and "Work in Progress."

Safety Mustard

Safety Yellow triggers high focus. By using it as our Primary, I signaled that the app isn't a "Playground"—it's a "Construction Site" for their future.

07 / The Gatekeeper

Designing for Trust.

Parents are the decision-makers. If they say "No," the product dies. I designed a Passive Trust Layer that sends data, scope, and progress videos directly to parents to turn their "Anxiety" into "Proof."
